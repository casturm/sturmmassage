About

01

Bodywork rooted in presence, not protocol.

I'm Chris Sturm, a licensed massage therapist based in Portland. My work centers on the nervous system — helping people downshift out of chronic activation, recover from the demands of an active life, and find their way back to ease in their own bodies.

I came to massage therapy after 25 years in tech, drawn by a simple truth: bodies need skilled, intentional touch to regulate, restore, and heal. Every session is shaped by real-time feedback — not a fixed routine — so the work meets you where you actually are.

I trained at East West College of the Healing Arts in Portland. My background in movement, yoga, and somatic awareness informs how I listen and how I work.

Approach

Your body isn't a problem to solve. It's a system to listen to.

Most of us live in a state of chronic low-grade activation — nervous systems running hot, bodies braced against the next thing. Therapeutic massage isn't just about muscle tension. It's about helping your system remember how to downshift.

I work with presence and precision, adapting to real-time feedback rather than following a fixed sequence. Sessions can be quiet and deeply restorative, or targeted and clinical — often both within the same hour.

If there's an injury or structural issue involved, I bring that into the frame too. But I don't let the clinical part override the relational one. The nervous system is always part of the picture.
